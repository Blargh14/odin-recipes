ICE-ED WATER

Description

The ever elusive dish known as ice inside of water.

Tall tales are told of master chefs who are able to use a single substance, but alternate the temperature of it to have it exist in two different states, creating a vastly superior form of water.

With modern technology however, we will let the machine do it. Then assemble it ourselves.

Ingredients

  • Water below 32 degrees Fahrenheit, shaped to your liking. Quanity variable.
  • Water above 32 degrees Fahrenheit, shape fluid. Quantity variable.
    •

    Steps

    1. Stare at icemaking device. Could be a fridge or any temperature control device rated below 32 F.
    2. Once device makes clinking noises, remove the amount of ice you would like to use.
    3. Fill container of your choice with dihydrogen monoxide, colloquially known as "water". Be careful on this step, as ice will add volume.
    4. Add ice to dihydrogen monoxide.
